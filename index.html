Australian Support & Crisis Contacts

If you need to talk to someone right now, or are looking for professional support in Australia, these services are available 24/7 or during specific hours. Don't hesitate to reach out.

Lifeline

24/7 crisis support and suicide prevention services.

Phone: 13 11 14

Text: 0477 13 11 14 (6pm-Midnight AEST)

Chat: lifeline.org.au/get-help/online-chat/ (7pm-Midnight AEST)

Kids Helpline

Free, confidential 24/7 online and phone counseling for young people aged 5 to 25.

Phone: 1800 55 1800

WebChat: kidshelpline.com.au/get-help/webchat-counselling

Beyond Blue

Offers mental health support, advice, and referrals for all ages.

Phone: 1300 22 4636

Chat: beyondblue.org.au/get-support/get-immediate-support

Headspace

National youth mental health foundation for 12-25 year olds, offering counseling and support.

Phone: 1800 650 890 (9am-1am AEST/AEDT, 7 days)

Online Chat: eheadspace

Find a Centre: headspace.org.au/our-locations/

Banksia Park International High School Contacts

Your school community at Banksia Park International High School is here to support you. If you need to talk to someone, or need guidance on school-related issues, please reach out to one of the following key people:

Year Level Leaders

Your Year Level Leader is a great first point of contact for any concerns related to your year level, academic progress, or general well-being within school.

How to contact: Speak to them directly, or ask at Student Services.

Wellbeing Team

The Wellbeing Team, located within Student Services, can provide confidential support and connect you with internal or external mental health resources.

How to contact: Visit Student Services, or ask a trusted teacher for assistance.

Teachers

Your teachers are here to help and can be a good starting point if you're comfortable. They can provide support or direct you to the right person.

How to contact: Approach them after class, or send them an email via the school system.
